<about>

    I was born and raised in rural North Carolina, in the foothills of the Appalachian Mountains. From an early age, I had an affinity for the arts, particularly music, and went to school at UNC-Greensboro for music performance. Quickly learning that my degree had little to no real-world benefit, I left, and worked menial service-industry jobs before finally becoming a full-time touring guitarist in Nashville, Tennessee. This lasted the better part of a decade, until the winter of 2018, when I suffered a nervous system malfunction that put an end to my physically demanding career. I spent the next year in recovery, trying to find anything that could fill the hole left by the loss of my obsession. I finally found this in computer programming.

    I began by learning the basics of programming and computational thinking with Python, completing the MITx course, "Introduction to Computer Science and Programming Using Python". I then completed the HarvardX course, "CS50's Introduction to Computer Science", which introduces computer science concepts such as memory management and data structures with C, then steps into some familiar territory with Python, finally moving on to web programming with HTML, CSS, and SQL using Flask. Since web programming seemed to provide the best opportunities for someone self-taught, I went through the freeCodeCamp.org curriculum, earning their Full Stack certification, (MERN stack), with sub-certifications in Responsive Web Design, JavaScript Algorithms and Data Structures, Front End Libraries, Data Visualization, APIs and Microservices, and Information Security and Quality Assurance. I'm now looking to launch a new career with the skills I've acquired.
